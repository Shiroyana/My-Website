# Cold Outreach — Scripts & Templates

Two channels: in-person/phone (best for local trades — plumbers, HVAC,
electricians answer their phone) and email (better for restaurants and
anyone whose site has a contact form or listed email). Adapted from the
CodeStitch freelancing guide's approach: lead with authenticity, diagnose
before you pitch, low financial risk, let them ask the questions.

## Ground rules

- **Never open with "How are you?"** — reads as a sales call immediately,
  triggers the brush-off reflex.
- **Lead with what you found**, not what you're selling. You're not
  cold-calling, you're following up on something specific you noticed.
- **Don't negotiate on the first contact.** The goal of contact #1 is to
  book the audit call, nothing else.
- **Mention the free audit early.** It's the lowest-risk way for a
  skeptical owner to say yes.

---

## Phone / in-person opener

> "Hey, is this [Name]? I'm Yan — I build websites for [plumbing/HVAC/
> restaurant] businesses in the area. I took a look at [Business]'s site
> and put together a quick, free audit — a couple things that might be
> costing you calls. Got two minutes, or is there a better time?"

If they ask "what's wrong with it":

> "Nothing's *broken* exactly — it's more that [specific thing: it's not
> mobile-friendly / it loads slow / there's no way to request a quote
> online / it doesn't show up when someone searches "[service] near me"].
> That's usually worth more in missed calls than it costs to fix. Want me
> to send over what I found?"

If they're busy:

> "No worries — when's better, later today or tomorrow?"

(Never suggest a specific time yourself first — let them anchor it.)

## Email version

**Subject:** Quick note about [Business Name]'s website

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm Yan, I build websites for [plumbing/HVAC/electrical/roofing/
> restoration/restaurant] businesses in [area]. I took a look at
> [Business]'s site and noticed [one specific, real observation — e.g.
> "it's not showing up for 'emergency plumber [city]' on Google" or "there's
> no way to request a quote without calling during business hours"].
>
> I put together a short, free audit — no pitch, just what I'd fix and
> why. Worth a quick look?
>
> — Yan
> Cambi Growth

## The "what makes you different" answer

When they ask why you vs. Wix or another local guy — answer with specifics,
not vibes:

- Hand-built, not a page-builder template — faster load times, no bloat
- [Site X] loads in under a second — [competitor sites are often 3-5x slower]
- Sites are actually built to convert: click-to-call, quote forms, review
  showcases — not just a digital brochure
- You're not buying a page, you're buying a system — SEO, automation, and
  ongoing optimization are part of every package, not a one-and-done

## Objection: "We already have a website"

> "Totally fair — a lot of what I do is fixing what's already there, not
> starting from scratch. The free audit works the same way either way: I'll
> tell you what's working, what's not, and what it's probably costing you.
> No obligation either way."

## Objection: "How much does this cost?"

Don't quote a number cold. Redirect to the audit/call:

> "Depends on what you need — I've got options starting at $0 down. Easier
> to give you a real number after I see what you're working with. Want to
> grab 15 minutes this week?"

## Booking the call

Once they agree, lock a specific time immediately (don't let it go to "I'll
follow up") and send a calendar invite or confirmation text same-day.

## Target list — where to find leads

1. Search Google Maps for `"[plumber/HVAC/electrician/roofer/restoration/
   restaurant] near [city]"` — sort by most reviews (established business,
   likely to have budget) or fewest (newer, may be more price-sensitive
   but hungrier).
2. Click into each listing's website. Red flags that mean opportunity:
   - No website at all (just a Google Business Profile)
   - GoDaddy/Wix/Squarespace free-tier URL, or a `.business.site` domain
   - Not mobile-friendly (check on your phone)
   - No clear way to request a quote or book online
   - Hasn't been updated in years (old copyright date, outdated design)
3. Check recent reviews — a business with good reviews and a bad website
   is the best-fit prospect: the reputation-gap pitch is literally true
   for them.
4. Log every prospect in a spreadsheet: business name, phone, contact
   name (if findable), current site quality (1-5), date contacted, and
   outcome. Follow up once if no response after ~5 business days — don't
   follow up more than twice without a reason to.
